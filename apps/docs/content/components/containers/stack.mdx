`Stack` is a `<div>` configured as `flex flex-col` with consistent
`gap`, `align`, and `justify` props. It is the right primitive any time
you have a list of items flowing top-to-bottom: card contents, form
sections, settings rows, vertical menus.

For row layouts use `Flex`. For two-axis grids use `Grid`. For one-off
styled divs without layout intent use `Box`.

## Anatomy

```
Stack
  gap?     "none" | "xs" | "sm" | "md" | "lg" | "xl" | "2xl"   (default "md")
  align?   "start" | "center" | "end" | "stretch" | "baseline"  (default "stretch")
  justify? "start" | "center" | "end" | "between" | "around" | "evenly" (default "start")
```

## Best practices

- **Pick a gap token, not a pixel.** Stack's `gap` prop maps to the
  design-system spacing scale (`gap-1` through `gap-12`). Free-form
  spacing belongs in a one-off styled div, not in a layout primitive.
- **Default `align="stretch"`.** Children fill the cross-axis - good
  for cards and form sections. Switch to `start` when items have
  varying widths and you want left alignment.
- **Reach for `justify` only when distributing space.** `between`,
  `around`, `evenly` matter for vertical menus and pricing rows;
  otherwise the default `start` is what you want.
